# TacticalReadinessFOB
Abstract:  
This study proposes the Tactical Readiness Index (TRI) as a measure of the operational readiness of Forward Operating Bases (FOBs), providing defense planners with a useful comparative tool. FOBs are often isolated and vulnerable, yet no standardized metric currently exists to evaluate and improve their capabilities. Having defined TRI, this research investigates whether readiness can be enhanced through autonomous resupply protocols. To address this question, we conduct Monte Carlo simulations that account for the statistical nature of key variables, including fuel and ammunition requirements, convoy delays, threat occurrences, and failures of autonomous systems due to weather, communication loss, or electronic interference. Results indicate that autonomy significantly improves readiness across most bases, reducing detection latency, enhancing mission continuity, shortening response times, and increasing resilience. However, the findings also reveal limitations: under conditions resembling heavy electronic warfare, the gains are markedly reduced. Future work will extend this framework to multi-domain operations within complex adaptive systems.
